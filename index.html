Dipping my toes into Web Development with the creation of this Site 1.0

3rd January 2021

As soon as I realised I might end up creating a website, I started taking note of what I was doing. Initially, inspiration wasn’t web development per se, however my desire to learn JavaScript led to me dipping my toes into the WebDev world and here we are. Hooray... Since everything’s recorded and I have further plans for this site and my skills as a developer, writing up how I got here could prove valuable in the future.

I started with the Odin Project and it was from here I started learning the basics of HTML & CSS (literally all I know is basics right now) with the best secondary resources as discovered through the Odin project, being Shayhowe.com and Mozilla Web Docs.

Once I reached the task of recreating the Google homepage as part of the Odin Project, I decided to opt for something where I could display more of my own creative flair with the new skills I had learnt. And since I have plans to write articles anyway, having recently written ‘Addressing the Why for Bitcoin’ I thought a blog would do nicely.

Thinking forward, as I intended this to be a Website I would in fact use, I didn’t like the idea of having to edit the code every time I wanted to publish a new article. I don’t hardcode tweets onto twitter; I log in, there’s a nice little section for tweeting and then I go spew foolishness. Here I realised a CMS or content management system might be better for my needs. Something like WordPress. However, since that would defeat the purpose of my Web-Development project I decided to go along with the process only marginally jaded. Focusing on styling and a framework for which more could be added.

Starting with style I first wanted to create a dark website, black background white text. However, the large majority of the Blogs I looked at for inspiration, the ones I read, were all white and I wondered if there was any true benefit. And unsurprisingly Dark UI isn’t the best choice when it comes to Blog-type content.

Having lost more than half of my brand identity I started thinking about the design of my blog and as I had learnt online decided to sketch it out on paper first. The name Un_Defined came to me by way of divine intervention, as a perfect fit to replace my placeholder/typo of UNdecided in my HTML.

When it came to fleshing out the page two YouTube videos were particularly helpful. My Nav-Bar Master in addition to my responsive CSS Grid God were my two angels, for without them entering my life, I may well have regressed and created a mock-up Google Homepage instead.

Once the site was fully marked up and the styling had been added I looked for ways to publish it. Through Mozilla Web Docs I learnt that you could actually do this through GitHub, which would be a perfect fit since I could populate my empty GitHub in the absence of a Google Homepage replica.

When you publish through GitHub pages your website must be static rather than dynamic. In looking at the differences between Static and Dynamic websites I began to see the power in Html and CSS and why writing it off for the use of CMS can only stem from ignorance. Further plans for this site actually include developing it into the latter to add more functionality and depth.

At this stage I wanted my own domain name rather than the bulky domain name as offered by GitHub. Seeing that you could add your own domain name to your GitHub hosted website was great and I eventually did this, not through google domains, but through the first few YouTube results’ favourite in Dynadot. ‘undefined.london’ was a nice find and was a good enough price in my opinion. ‘.com’ and the other favourites were taken but this works perfectly for now.

I was practically finished here, however I started to question the negatives to hosting with GitHub; It seemed too good of a deal. In learning about some of the downsides of control, speed, security, I realised they weren’t particularly impactful to me right now. The next iteration of this site would solve these perhaps as I move off of GitHub and to a paid host.

Speaking of, the 1.0 in the title is in no way meaningless I have full intention of adding to and quite dramatically changing the way this site operates and furthermore, writing about it. I will use this site and my desire to upgrade it, not only as incentive in becoming a full stack developer but also as a vessel for the many ideas that require its growth from a static Web blog hosted by GitHub to whatever mutation I’ve fathered in at 9.0.

Next on The Odin Project for me: Rock, Paper, Scissors in JavaScript. Hooray...

Edit: So unfortunately the site as I has had setup from this point was not running. I was getting this message when I believed I should be able to access it. Looking back at how to point a google domain to GitHub pages I realised I was missing the step involving any IP address (GitHub’s). And as much as it brings pangs about mine heart the easiest way to rectify this issue was to cave in to Big Tech and use Google Domains just like the tutorial. However the DynaDot UI wasn’t half as clear or easy to use. Google Domains should’ve been a no-brainer maybe?

Also, when troubleshooting, the GitHub Docs page already had everything outlined. But I had overlooked it. The lesson learnt here being the documentation provided should probably be checked regardless of whether you believe you know what you’re doing or not. In this case I could’ve saved myself from unnecessarily buying undefined.london which I still own; I have no doubt overspent now. However since undefined.london is mine for the year and I prefer it to nicodeth.me, I might switch at some point with some more understanding.
